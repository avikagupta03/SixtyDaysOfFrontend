Machine Learning

Involves algorithms that enable systems to learn from data and make predictions or decisions based on it. ML models improve automatically over time with more data, making them ideal for tasks like image recognition, recommendation systems, and fraud detection.

Deep Learning

A branch of machine learning that uses large neural networks with multiple layers (deep networks) to model and solve complex problems. Deep learning excels in applications like image and speech recognition, natural language processing, and game AI, by analyzing large datasets.

Computer Vision

Focuses on enabling machines to interpret and understand visual data from the world. It includes tasks like image recognition, object detection, facial recognition, and video analysis, commonly used in autonomous vehicles, medical imaging, and surveillance systems.

Natural Language Processing (NLP)

Deals with the interaction between computers and human languages. NLP enables machines to process, understand, and generate human language, powering applications such as chatbots, translation services, sentiment analysis, and voice assistants.

Robotics

Combines AI and mechanical engineering to create robots that can perform tasks autonomously or semi-autonomously. AI enhances robotic abilities in areas like navigation, task execution, and interaction with environments, as seen in drones, industrial robots, and healthcare robots.

Expert Systems

AI programs designed to mimic the decision-making abilities of human experts in specific fields. These systems use a set of rules derived from expert knowledge to solve complex problems, commonly used in fields like medical diagnosis, customer support, and troubleshooting.

Speech Recognition

Converts spoken language into text, allowing machines to understand human speech. This technology is behind virtual assistants like Siri and Alexa, automated transcription services, and voice-activated systems, enabling hands-free interactions.

AI Planning

Involves creating algorithms that allow machines to plan a sequence of actions to achieve a specific goal. AI planning is used in fields like robotics for task scheduling, logistics, and autonomous navigation in complex environments.

Fuzzy Logic

Helps systems handle imprecise or uncertain data, allowing for more flexible decision-making. Unlike binary logic (true/false), fuzzy logic works with degrees of truth, making it ideal for control systems in appliances, medical diagnosis, and decision-making under uncertainty.
